# Limitations and Scope Constraints

This document is drafted **first**, before any simulator code, datasets, or
models exist, and is treated as a living reference: every later phase is
checked against it, and any proposal that would require loosening one of
these constraints must be flagged explicitly and discussed before proceeding.

## 1. What this paper is, and is not

- This is an **applied / computer-engineering** paper: a comparative study of
  existing deep learning architectures on an engineering-evaluation axis
  (training cost, inference latency, parameter count, robustness, anomaly-score
  behavior), plus an interpretability layer.
- It is **not** a methods-novelty paper. No new architecture is proposed.
- It is **not** a nuclear-safety-validation paper. No claim is made about the
  safety performance of any real reactor, RBMK or otherwise.
- It is **not** a simulator-novelty paper. The reactor kinetics simulator is
  an assembly of existing, cited, peer-reviewed formulations (point kinetics
  with six delayed-neutron groups, Xe-135/I-135 poisoning dynamics, RBMK-style
  void-coefficient feedback), not a new physics model.

## 2. Data

- **All training and evaluation data is synthetic**, generated by the
  literature-based kinetics simulator described in `/simulator/DESIGN_NOTE.md`.
- **No real plant telemetry of any kind (RBMK, PWR, or otherwise) is used for
  training or evaluation.** Strong performance on the synthetic dataset is
  **not** evidence of performance on real plant telemetry. This sim-to-real
  gap applies independently of the Chernobyl framing below and must be stated
  explicitly in the paper's limitations section.

## 3. The Chernobyl case study — the central firewall

- The digitized Chernobyl pre-accident trajectory (from INSAG-7, IAEA reports,
  and peer-reviewed RBMK kinetics reconstructions) is used **only** as an
  **illustrative, qualitative** test sequence run against the already-trained
  models.
- It is **not** training data.
- It is **not** used to set, fit, or recalibrate any simulator parameter.
  Simulator parameters are sourced independently from the reactor-physics
  literature (Source Registry IDs B1–B9) and frozen before dataset generation.
- The "Anchor Registry" (`/literature/anchor_registry.*`) contains only
  **qualitative** historical facts (e.g. "ORM fell below the required minimum
  before the test", "an iodine pit was present at the time of the test").
  These anchors may be used to ask "does the simulator's general behavior look
  physically reasonable given this?" during plausibility checks (Phase 6) and
  the domain sanity check (Phase 9) — **never** as numeric targets, ranges, or
  loss terms.
- If a plausibility check against an anchor fails, any resulting change to a
  simulator parameter must cite an **independent** justification from the
  Source Registry (B1–B9). "To better match the historical trace" is **never**
  an acceptable justification, and any such change must be logged.

## 4. Claims that are explicitly out of scope

The following claims, in any form, are **forbidden** anywhere in the paper,
code comments, figure captions, or supplementary material:

- "This system would have prevented the Chernobyl accident" or any equivalent
  prevention / counterfactual safety claim.
- Any claim that the AZ-5 positive-scram / graphite-displacer-tip effect could
  be addressed, mitigated, or detected by a monitoring or neural-network
  layer. This was a core mechanical/design flaw, independent of information
  systems, and is explicitly **not modeled** by the simulator (see Validation
  Registry, item V04).
- Any quantitative "the model would have alarmed N minutes before the scram"
  claim derived from the digitized Chernobyl data.
- Any claim of novelty for the underlying RBMK physics model or the assembled
  simulator.
- Any claim that the interpretability method is the **first** application of
  XAI/SHAP/attention to reactor or nuclear telemetry. The correct framing is
  narrower: **cross-architecture validation of interpretability outputs
  against known, injected, ground-truth anomaly sources**, within a
  comparative engineering study.

## 5. Architecture scope

- Four architecture families are compared: LSTM Autoencoder, GRU
  Encoder-Decoder, CNN/Wavelet-based, Transformer-based.
- Graph Attention Network / graph-based models are **out of scope** (closed
  off explicitly; not deferred, not "future work" in a way that implies
  partial completion).
- The specific CNN/Wavelet variant is **not yet fixed** — see open items in
  the roadmap. Until it is fixed, no claims comparing "the CNN/Wavelet
  architecture" should be written into the paper.

## 6. Digitization and provenance

- Every digitized data point used in the Chernobyl case study must carry
  documented provenance: source document, digitization tool, estimated
  pixel-to-value error.
- Where possible, each digitized variable/timestamp is cross-validated against
  a second independent source.
- "Ghost reading" artifacts (gridline bleed-through, compression artifacts,
  interpolation smoothing, cross-contamination between overlapping plotted
  curves) must be explicitly checked for and flagged.
- Any digitized point that cannot be corroborated is marked uncertain or
  excluded — never silently used.

## 7. Reactor physics simplifications (to be reconciled, not hidden)

- The simulator uses **point kinetics**, not spatial/3D neutronics. This is a
  standard simplification in the literature (B1–B3) and must be stated as
  such.
- The six-delayed-neutron-group precursor data commonly used in point-kinetics
  models (Keepin, 1965) is derived for thermal fission of U-235. RBMK fuel
  with significant Pu-239/241 burnup has a measurably different effective
  delayed-neutron fraction. How this is reconciled (or not) must be stated
  explicitly in `/simulator/DESIGN_NOTE.md` and reviewed at the domain sanity
  check (Phase 9) — it must not be silently combined as-is.
- The AZ-5 / positive-scram / graphite-displacer transient is **not modeled**.
  This is a deliberate scope decision (Section 4), recorded as Validation
  Registry item V04, not an oversight.
